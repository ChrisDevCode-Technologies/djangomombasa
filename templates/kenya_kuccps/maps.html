Glossary

Definitions of key terms used on this page.

KUCCPSAbbreviation

Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service — the government body that coordinates student placement into universities, colleges, and TVET institutions based on KCSE performance.

KCSEAbbreviation

Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education — the national examination taken at the end of secondary school. KCSE grades determine eligibility for university and college placement.

County

One of Kenya's 47 administrative and political subdivisions established by the 2010 Constitution. Each county hosts one or more educational institutions. The maps shade counties by how many institutions or offerings they contain.

Institution

A university, constituent college, or TVET institution registered with KUCCPS that offers degree, diploma, certificate, or artisan programmes. Each institution belongs to a single county and is classified by type (e.g. Public, Private) and category (e.g. University, National Polytechnic).

Programme

A named course of study (e.g. "Bachelor of Science in Computer Science") that a student can be placed into. Each programme belongs to one cluster/programme group and may be offered by multiple institutions.

Programme Offering

A specific instance of a programme at a particular institution. For example, "Computer Science at University of Nairobi" is one offering, while "Computer Science at Kenyatta University" is another — each with its own cut-off point.

ClusterAlso: Programme Group

A grouping of related programmes that share similar KCSE subject requirements. Examples include "Biological Sciences", "Engineering", or "Business and Related Studies". The Cluster Intelligence map shows how offerings for each cluster are distributed across counties.

Cut-off Point

The minimum KCSE aggregate score required for placement into a specific programme offering in a given year. Cut-off points vary by institution and programme, and change from year to year based on demand and available slots.

TVETAbbreviation

Technical and Vocational Education and Training — institutions offering hands-on diploma, certificate, and artisan-level programmes in fields such as engineering, ICT, hospitality, and agriculture.

Choropleth Map

A map where geographic areas (counties) are shaded in proportion to a data value. Darker green shading indicates higher counts (more institutions or offerings), while lighter shading indicates lower counts.
